Crown Shyness

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Crown shyness (also canopy disengagement ,[1] canopy shyness, [2] or inter-crown spacing) is a feature observed in some tree species, in which the crowns of fully stocked trees do not touch each other, instead forming a canopy with channel-like gaps. [4][5] This is most prevalent among trees of the same species, but also occurs between trees of different species. [6][7] There exist many hypotheses as to why crown shyness is an adaptive behavior, and research suggests that it might inhibit spread of leaf-eating insect larvae. [8]
